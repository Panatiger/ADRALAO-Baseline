Nature-Based Social Inclusive Livelihood and Climate Action Project (NaBCAP)

Baseline Survey Insights: A Snapshot of Kham District

Understanding the Communities

The NaBCAP project baseline survey interviewed 378 vulnerable smallholder farming households in the Kham District, Xiengkhouang Province. This baseline analysis establishes the starting point for measuring project success in fostering sustainable economic growth, environmental protection, and resilience to climate change. The data reveals a population facing significant economic and social challenges, with a strong reliance on agriculture and natural resources.

Household Wellbeing Status

A significant majority of households (60.3%) are classified as poor, underscoring the critical need for livelihood interventions.

Outcome 1: Livelihood Opportunities

The economic landscape is challenging, with low agricultural productivity and limited access to markets and capital. The data highlights a pressing need to diversify income streams and build resilience, particularly for women and people with disabilities.

Primary Livelihood Sources

Households are heavily reliant on crop and livestock production, making them vulnerable to climate shocks. There is a clear need to strengthen these traditional sectors while fostering alternative income sources.

Perceived Change in Yields (Past Year)

Less than a third of households reported increased yields, with women and PWD households experiencing the least improvement, highlighting deep-seated inequities in access to resources and technology.

Top Challenges in Income Generation

The primary barriers to economic growth are systemic, focusing on a lack of opportunities, market access, skills, and capital. Interventions must address these interconnected challenges to be effective.

Outcome 2: Land & Water Management

Communities depend heavily on their natural environment, yet face significant challenges from deforestation, resource degradation, and insecure land tenure. Strengthening community-based NRM is crucial for long-term sustainability.

Outcome 3: Inclusion & Climate Action Services

Social and climate resilience are deeply intertwined. The baseline reveals significant barriers preventing vulnerable groups from participating in community life and accessing vital climate information, limiting their capacity to adapt.

Capacity to Advocate for Needs

Advocacy capacity is alarmingly low, especially for women, who are often left out of decision-making processes. Empowering these voices is essential for equitable development.
